Picture actual fishing: you trick a fish into biting your hook by baiting them with something they want, like a worm. Phishing (with a “ph”) is a similar concept. The scammer disguises a dangerous message—often with the aim of getting you to click a link or download an attachment—by pretending to be a sender you know. This can look like a sale offer from a place you shop at, a note from your employer or an "urgent message" from a company like Amazon or PayPal. Phishing can be wide-spread, or target specific groups or individuals, like employees of the same company, which is called "spear phishing."
